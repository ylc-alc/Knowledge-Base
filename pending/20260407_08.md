# Knowledge Summary
A strategic argument against "skill bloating" in AI agents, advocating for high-quality, single-purpose skills rather than a large quantity of mediocre plugins. [cite: 4]

# Extracted Details
- **The Problem**: Having too many skills (e.g., 40+) can decrease the overall intelligence and efficiency of an AI agent due to confusion and interference. [cite: 4]
- **Amazon Metaphor**: Amazon's approach to plugins emphasizes that prompt quality is the core "product," and skills are just containers for that logic. [cite: 4]
- **Plugin Management**: Effective plugins require deep domain knowledge; simply copying third-party prompts often leads to poor performance. [cite: 4]

# Key Takeaways
- Focus on developing one "super-skill" that works reliably rather than dozens of unstable ones. [cite: 4]
- Treat AI skills as tools for professional workflows where precision is more important than breadth. [cite: 4]

# Actionable Tips
- When creating a plugin, ensure it is built on first-hand professional experience ("Know-how") rather than just surface-level prompt logic. [cite: 4]
