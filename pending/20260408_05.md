# Knowledge Summary
A strategic guide on using AI (specifically Claude) to prepare for annual performance reviews by translating work logs into persuasive achievements and anticipating management pushback [cite: 2].

# Extracted Details
- The core of a performance review is not just what you did, but how well you can **make others believe in what you did** [cite: 2].
- **Prompt 1 (Persuasive List):** Asks Claude to reformat work logs using the **"Action + Result + Influence"** format and identify missing data [cite: 2].
- **Prompt 2 (Core Contribution):** Asks Claude to identify the single most valuable contribution that can be explained to a manager in **30 seconds** [cite: 2].
- **Prompt 3 (Predicting Doubts):** Asks Claude to roleplay as a **strict, data-driven manager** to list 3 potential questions/doubts and prepare response strategies [cite: 2].
- **Prompt 4 (Future Planning):** Asks Claude to draft ambitious but realistic goals for the following year to demonstrate direction and planning [cite: 2].

# Key Takeaways
- Success in reviews often comes from being **data-driven and story-oriented** [cite: 2].
- Preparation should ideally start **two weeks in advance** to avoid vague claims like "I worked hard this year" [cite: 2].

# Actionable Tips
- When preparing Prompt 1, provide Claude with a bulleted list of your raw tasks to ensure it has enough context to apply the "Influence" framework [cite: 2].
- Use the output of Prompt 3 to conduct a "mock interview" with yourself or a peer to build confidence [cite: 2].
