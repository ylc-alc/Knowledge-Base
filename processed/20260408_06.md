# Knowledge Summary
A LinkedIn post discussing recent revelations from Google regarding historical inaccuracies in Google Search Console (GSC) impressions data and the resulting distrust within the SEO community [cite: 3].

# Extracted Details
- **Google Announcement:** Google stated that GSC impressions data has been inaccurate since May 13, 2025 [cite: 3].
- **Google's Claim:** They maintain that "Clicks and other metrics were not affected by the error" and that the issue was limited to data logging [cite: 3].
- **Historical Context:** The post references an "&num=100" issue that occurred around September 2025, which the author initially suspected was related to artificial impression boosting or LLM/SERP tracker activity [cite: 3].
- **Timeline Concern:** It took nearly a year for Google to inform website owners that the data was tainted [cite: 3].
- **Metric Interdependence:** The author argues that GSC metrics do not work in isolation; over-reporting impressions directly impacts Click-Through Rate (CTR) and average ranking position [cite: 3].

# Key Takeaways
- **Data Integrity:** The delay in reporting data errors undermines the trust SEO professionals place in official Google reporting tools [cite: 3].
- **Downstream Effects:** Inaccurate impression counts lead to skewed CTR calculations, which can misrepresent the performance of SEO strategies [cite: 3].

# Actionable Tips
- **Audit Historical Data:** Note the period starting May 13, 2025, in your internal reporting to account for potentially inflated impression numbers and deflated CTRs [cite: 3].
- **Diversify Tracking:** Avoid relying solely on GSC for performance measurement; use third-party tools to cross-reference ranking positions and traffic data [cite: 3].
