# Knowledge Summary
Detailed breakdown of the differences between Search Engine Optimisation (SEO), Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO), and Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO), including their mechanics, pros, and cons. [cite: 1]

# Extracted Details
- **SEO (Search Engine Optimisation)**:
    - Goal: Get a website to appear in traditional search results. [cite: 1]
    - Method: High-intent keywords and authoritative backlinks. [cite: 1]
    - Pros: Proven traffic driver; builds long-term authority. [cite: 1]
    - Cons: Slow results; vulnerable to algorithm updates. [cite: 1]
- **AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)**:
    - Goal: Extract expertise into answer boxes and AI Overviews. [cite: 1]
    - Method: Direct answers to client questions and FAQ/How-to schema. [cite: 1]
    - Pros: Instant authority; strong for voice and mobile queries. [cite: 1]
    - Cons: Users may get answers without clicking (zero-click); snippet positions change frequently. [cite: 1]
- **GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation)**:
    - Goal: Get cited by name in detailed AI-generated recommendations (e.g., ChatGPT). [cite: 1]
    - Method: Publish factual, well-structured thought leadership with citations and E-E-A-T principles. [cite: 1]
    - Pros: Early-mover advantage in AI search; positions firm as a trusted authority. [cite: 1]
    - Cons: Hard to measure direct impact; requires presence on multiple platforms beyond your own website. [cite: 1]

# Actionable Tips
- **To Rank Higher in AEO**: Answer questions in concise 40-60 word blocks under clear headings. [cite: 1]
- **To Rank Higher in GEO**: Contribute to sources AI models frequently reference, such as industry reports, forums, and publications. [cite: 1]
