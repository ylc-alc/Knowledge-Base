# Knowledge Summary
A simple narrative structure designed to make communication more engaging by introducing conflict and resolution rather than just listing facts.

# Extracted Details
- 'AND' sets the stage: Establishes the current context or status.
- 'BUT' introduces conflict: Highlights a challenge or change that grabs attention.
- 'THEREFORE' delivers resolution: Provides the solution or a clear next step.
- Example: 'We are on track AND adoption is rising, BUT a client paused spending, THEREFORE I am preparing a follow-up call.'

# Key Takeaways
- Moving away from 'And, And, And' (AAA) structures prevents audience boredom.
- Conflict is the 'engine' of narrative that makes people 'lean in'.

# Actionable Tips
- Apply the ABT structure to status reports and emails to clarify the problem-solving process.
