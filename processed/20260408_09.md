# Knowledge Summary
An extensive bibliography focused on the historical development of capitalism, political economy, and institutional growth, with specific emphasis on Taiwan's economic transition and contemporary corporate power [cite: 2].

# Extracted Details
- **Foundations of Capitalism**:
    - *15th to 18th Century Material Civilization* (Fernand Braudel): Long-run economic history [cite: 2].
    - *A Brief History of Neoliberalism* (David Harvey): Post-late 20th-century economic shifts [cite: 2].
    - *Capital, Volume 1* (Karl Marx): Critique of capital and labor exploitation [cite: 2].
- **Institutional & Global Development**:
    - *Why Nations Fail* (Acemoglu & Robinson): The role of inclusive institutions in prosperity [cite: 2].
    - *Guns, Germs, and Steel* (Jared Diamond): Geographical and environmental influence on power [cite: 2].
- **Taiwan-Specific Analysis**:
    - *Privilege of Wealth* (致富的特權): A critique of Taiwan's central bank policies [cite: 2].
    - *Unfinished Miracle* (未竟的奇蹟): Sociological analysis of Taiwan's economic miracle and structural transition [cite: 2].
- **Modern Corporate & Digital Power**:
    - *Empire of Accounting* (會計帝國): Investigation into the monopoly of the Big Four accounting firms [cite: 2].
    - *Technofeudalism* (Yanis Varoufakis): Economic analysis of platform capitalism [cite: 2].

# Key Takeaways
- To understand modern prosperity, one must analyze the institutional frameworks (Acemoglu) and historical silver/trade flows (林滿紅) that shaped global power [cite: 2].
- Digital platforms are argued to be shifting from standard capitalism toward a "cloud feudalism" order [cite: 2].

# Actionable Tips
- For a localized understanding of economic policy, read *Privilege of Wealth* to understand the trade-offs of Taiwan's central banking decisions [cite: 2].
- Start with *Why Nations Fail* to build a foundational understanding of how political systems dictate economic outcomes [cite: 2].
